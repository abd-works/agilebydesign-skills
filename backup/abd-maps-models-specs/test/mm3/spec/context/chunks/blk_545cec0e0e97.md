OUTCOME

Six  rounds  after  the  heroes  arrive  (or  as  the  GM  needs),
the ritual is completed. Even if the heroes evade the Avian
warriors and attempt to interfere with the Hekawi mystics,
they find the mystical energies in the area already cascad-
ing to a crescendo. The statue is anchored to the ground,
and the orb at its heart glows until the clearing is lit as if
it were midday.

In  the  seventh  round  (or  again,  as  the  GM  needs),  the
statue  begins  to  grow.  As  the  arch  beneath  the  wings
of the bird-gods grows, space and time tear beneath it,
forming a gate that swallows the light and sound from
the area, starting with the calls of the nighthawks from
the  surrounding  trees.  At  the  end  of  the  eighth  round,
the gateway is 30 feet tall and the now-enormous statue
is suddenly gone, drawn into the tear. But the gateway
remains, and the assembled Sons of the Sky immediately
move toward and through it (abandoning the battle if it
is still going on). If the heroes have immobilized any of
the Sons and attempt to question them, the Sons are still
caught up in the fervor of the ritual and can only screech,
“You  don’t  understand! We  go  through  the  veil  to  save
them! To save them!”

The  gateway  continues  to  absorb  more  sound  and  light
until  heroes  with  only  normal  senses  are  barely  able  to
perceive  their  surroundings  or  hear  yelled  conversation.
But the gateway remains stable. To recover the Fire—and
