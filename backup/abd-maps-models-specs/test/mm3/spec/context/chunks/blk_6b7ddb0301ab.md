CIRCUMSTANCE MODIFIERS

A good guideline to keep in mind is the chance of an aver-
age character (with a modifier of 0) succeeding at an av-
erage task (DC 10) is just over 50% (55% to be exact). So
any time you have to have an average character do some-
thing, or want to set a difficulty you feel is average for a
particular  character,  aim  for  around  that  chance  of  suc-

Circumstance modifiers (see their description in The Ba-
sics chapter) are one of the GM’s best tools. Rather than
having  to  memorize  a  lengthy  list  of  special-case  modi-
fiers,  just  keep  this  guideline  in  mind:  if  the  situation  is
in  the  character’s  favor,  that’s  good  for  a  +2  bonus  on  a
check.  If  it’s  against  the  character,  that’s  a  –2  penalty.  If
things are particularly good or bad, up the circumstance
modifier to +/–5.
