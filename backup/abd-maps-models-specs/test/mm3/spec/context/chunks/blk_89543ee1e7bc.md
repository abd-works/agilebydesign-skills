GRADED CHECKS

In  cases  where  two  or  more  characters  are  actively  op-
posing  each  other,  both  roll  checks  and  compare  the
results. In some situations, however, one or more of the
characters in an opposed check may not even be aware
of it! For example, a guard standing watch and looking
for intruders would make a Perception check to oppose
any  attempt  at  Stealth,  but  somebody  just  sitting  in  a
park, not expecting anyone to sneak up on her, isn’t spe-
cifically looking. This is a case of routine opposition, in

Much of the time a check is a simple pass-fail, it either suc-
ceeds,  or  it  does  not.  In  other  cases,  it  matters  just  how
well the check succeeded, or how badly it failed. This gra-
dation of results is called a graded check and involves a
degree of success or failure.
