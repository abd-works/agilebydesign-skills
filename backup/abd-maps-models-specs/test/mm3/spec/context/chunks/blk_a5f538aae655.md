COMPLICATIONS

•  Motivation—Acceptance: Due to the nature of their
powers, many Mimics feel as if they lack an identity of
their own. They may seek acceptance as unique indi-
viduals.

•

•

Addiction: A Mimic may actually need or somehow
derive sustenance from duplicating the traits of oth-
ers.

Quirk: Mimics may inadvertently absorb a portion of
their subject’s personality and even be overwhelmed
on  occasion,  forcing  the  Mimic  to  behave  like  their
subject.

•  Weakness: By duplicating their powers, Mimics may
acquire  the  weaknesses  and  vulnerabilities  of  their
subjects.
