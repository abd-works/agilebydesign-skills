THE MIDNIGHT MUSEUM

It’s three in the morning in Emerald City, and there’s been
a break-in at the city’s Native American Cultural Center.

Tonight was also the first night of Sound and Fury, Emer-
ald City’s annual music festival, and the E.C.P.D. is focused
on  maintaining  order  at  the  rowdy  opening  concert.  At
the end of the week, the festival will close with a concert
by  local  hip-hop  superstars  the  Longbowz  at  the  Native
American Cultural Center, a popular museum on the edge
of the Emerald City University campus dedicated to both
the  local  Hekawi  tribe  and  all  the  native  peoples  of  the
Pacific Northwest. Fearing a delayed or drawn-out inves-
tigation  by  the  police  might  lead  to  the  cancellation  of
the  concert,  the  Center’s  biggest  annual  fundraiser,  the
heroes are welcomed inside when they arrive by Professor
John Salinas, head of the Center.
