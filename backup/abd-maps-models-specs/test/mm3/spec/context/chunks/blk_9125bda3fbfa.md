THE HUCKSTER

Johnny  Goff  is  a  born  salesman,  maybe  the  best  who
ever lived. Selling ice to Eskimos was just another day’s
work for a man of his abilities. Johnny might have made
a respectable fortune selling quality wares, but the high
margin of profit for slipshod work called to him. It wasn’t
long  before  arrogance  and  greed  seduced  him  into
hawking dangerously defective products. Someone was
bound  to  get  hurt,  and  eventually  a  sales  trip  through
southwest Virginia  peddling  home-brewed “low-calorie
beer” left a lot of blind hillbillies in its wake. One class-
action  lawsuit  later,  Goff  was  left  with  nothing  but  a
warehouse full of fatally faulty merchandise.

Goff was always on the edge of criminality, and poverty
more  than  pushed  him  over  it.  Determined  to  rebuild
his lost fortune at any cost, he modified his hazardously
defective  gadgets  into  even  more  deadly  devices.  He
dubbed himself “the Huckster” after the traveling charla-
tans of old, adopting the title pinned upon him as a badge
of  honor.  Like  them,  the  Huckster  is  all  about  the  sales
pitch, but his spellbinding blarney is always a prelude to
much more blatant thefts.
