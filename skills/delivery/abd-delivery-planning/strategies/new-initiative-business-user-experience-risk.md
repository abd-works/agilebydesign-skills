# Strategy: New Initiative — Business / User Experience Risk

**When to use:** The main uncertainty is whether the **product is right** for people and operations — not (primarily) whether the tech stack integrates. Signals include: new **user experience**, unclear **domain** language and rules, risky **operational flows** (who does what, when, under which exceptions), and **data** that must be **correct in meaning** (not just stored). Greenfield or brownfield.

**Typical scope:** Riskiest **thin slices** first; prove **experience, domain, operations, and data** with the same **spec → test → code** rhythm as the proprietary-tech strategy.

---

Do rows **top to bottom**. **Outcome assessment** and **discovery revisit** run **only after** a thin slice is **through Engineering** (after **3d**); they do not run before the first delivery run.

### Combining with *Proprietary Technology Risk*

These two strategies are **often integrated or merged** in real plans. A thin slice can (and usually should) mix **technical risk** (undocumented systems, integrations) and **business risk** (UX, domain, operational flow, data meaning). **There is no single correct formula:** the **user** and the **delivery lead agent** decide how much of each risk type belongs in each slice and in what order. Use this strategy when business validation dominates; use *Proprietary Technology Risk* when integration dominates; **blend** them when both matter in the same initiative. See [new-initiative-proprietary-technology-risk.md](new-initiative-proprietary-technology-risk.md).
